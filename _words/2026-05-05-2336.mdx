title: "Standing on this side of the shift"
---
It's one of the most unique times in life. The before and after is as stark as any.

Right now on May 5th 2026, I have a significant control over how I spend my time. I can wake up when I want, work when I want, sleep when I want.

My work hours are flexible, and can shift from a standard work day to a late one.

But soon it all changes. Not the work, but what — and who — matters.

The wife is pregnant, and due to pop in under a month.

It's a strange feeling, standing at the end of a season like this.

Getting married was huge, especially for an overthinker like me.

But having a kid? That's another level.

The responsibility never ends.

I'm beyond excited, and can't wait to meet the little one.

But standing on this side of a shift like that, from having minimal responsibility to having so much, is humbling. Life will never go back to the way it is now.

So I'm taking a moment to take it in. What it feels like to just be me, for us to just be us.

Because soon things will change, hugely for the better, but with sacrifices too.

This selfish guy might just have to grow up.
